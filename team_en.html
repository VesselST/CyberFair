Students

李鵬成 Li Peng-Cheng

When I took Mr. Su Wen-Kui’s elective course for the first time, I learned about the International Schools CyberFair competition. We would need to build a website, which felt quite challenging. After careful discussion, we decided to participate. Although I did not contribute much to the website-building side, I was able to help with collecting information and writing part of the website content. On several occasions, we visited Chi Wen-Hao, the President of the Alumni Association. Through the interviews, I learned some basic reporting principles—for example, you must be clear about the topic, think through what questions to ask in advance, anticipate how the other person might respond, and, if the response is different from what you expected, adapt on the spot. That process also included some fun and unexpected moments. The chairman also shared a line with us: “Be able to yield, be able to endure, be able to teach, be able to wait” emphasizing perseverance, tolerance, and communication—exactly the life experience I still lack. This was an unprecedented competition experience for me. If I have the chance to participate again, I would like to do it again.
